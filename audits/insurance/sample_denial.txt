SYNTHETIC DENIAL LETTER — modeled on patterns documented in Estate of
Lokken v. UnitedHealth Group (D. Minn., Case No. 0:23-cv-03514) and
related class actions challenging algorithmic claim denials. All names,
member IDs, policy numbers, and dates are fabricated. The structure,
language, and clinical-policy citations are representative of denial
letters at issue in those public cases.

DETERMINATION: COVERAGE TERMINATED EFFECTIVE DAY 17.

We have reviewed your continued stay at the above skilled nursing
facility following discharge from inpatient hospitalization for hip
fracture (S72.001A) with surgical repair. Our coverage determination
applies the Medicare benefit policy at LCD L33635 and our predictive
length-of-stay model, which estimates that members with your clinical
profile typically require 16.4 days of skilled nursing care.

Based on this estimate and our review of the daily nursing notes, we
have determined that:

  1. Your therapy minutes have declined below the threshold required
     to demonstrate "active rehabilitation."
  2. Your functional improvement (as measured by Section GG of the
     MDS) has plateaued over the prior 3 days.
  3. The level of services you are receiving could, in our judgment,
     be safely provided in a lower level of care.

Accordingly, skilled nursing facility coverage for the dates of service
beginning on Day 17 and continuing is DENIED as not medically necessary
under the terms of your Medicare Advantage coverage.

You have the right to appeal this decision. You may request a fast-track
appeal through your state's Quality Improvement Organization (QIO)
within 24 hours of receiving this notice if you disagree with the
discharge date. Standard appeals must be filed within 60 days.

ATTACHED: Notice of Medicare Non-Coverage (NOMNC).

NOTE TO HUNT'S AUDITORS: This synthetic letter exhibits four denial-
defect patterns that the public Lokken complaint alleges UnitedHealth's
nH Predict tool systematically introduces:

  (a) Substitution of an algorithmic length-of-stay estimate for the
      individualized medical-necessity review required under 42 C.F.R.
      § 422.101(b) and CMS Medicare Managed Care Manual Ch. 4.
  (b) Reliance on LCD/NCD citations that do not, on their face, govern
      the clinical question (LCD L33635 is a wound-care policy; it does
      not establish the threshold for SNF medical necessity).
  (c) Citation of "therapy minute" and "Section GG plateau" criteria
      that the QIO standards for skilled-nursing termination expressly
      reject as the sole grounds for discharge.
  (d) Omission of the procedural disclosures required by ERISA § 503
      and 29 C.F.R. § 2560.503-1 for adverse benefit determinations
      under employer-sponsored Medicare Advantage plans — including the
      specific clinical rationale, the qualifications of the reviewer,
      and notice of the member's right to all documents relevant to
      the claim.

A Hunt specialist panel posting against this denial would be expected
to surface at least defects (a)-(c) via the medical-necessity and
state-external-review specialists, and defect (d) via the ERISA
specialist if the underlying plan is an employer-sponsored Medicare
Advantage product.
