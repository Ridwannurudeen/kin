SYNTHETIC SSDI DENIAL LETTER — patterns modeled on the standard SSA-1561
"Notice of Disapproved Claim" template and the publicly documented
medical-vocational decision-tree at 20 C.F.R. § 404.1505 et seq. All
names, SSNs, claim numbers, and dates are fabricated. The structure,
language, and reasoning patterns are representative of denial letters
the Social Security Administration issues at the initial determination
stage. Approximately 60-70% of initial SSDI applications are denied
each year; appeal-success rates with representation exceed 50% at the
ALJ hearing stage. As of January 2026, ~330,000 cases are pending an
ALJ hearing with an average wait of 274 days.

We are writing to tell you about the decision on the Social Security
disability claim you filed on [DATE].

WE FOUND THAT YOU ARE NOT DISABLED UNDER OUR RULES.

This decision was based on the information you and your doctors gave us.

  Information About Your Condition
  --------------------------------
  You said you cannot work because of degenerative disc disease of the
  lumbar spine, chronic radicular pain, controlled type 2 diabetes
  mellitus, and major depressive disorder. The medical evidence shows
  that you have these conditions. We considered the medical and other
  information, your age, education, training, and work experience in
  determining how your condition affects your ability to work.

  Listing of Impairments
  ----------------------
  We considered whether your conditions meet or medically equal any of
  the impairments listed in 20 C.F.R. Part 404, Subpart P, Appendix 1
  (the "Listing of Impairments"). The Listings describe, for each of
  the major body systems, impairments that are considered severe
  enough to prevent a person from doing any gainful activity. We
  determined that your conditions, considered singly and in
  combination, do not meet or medically equal the requirements of
  any listed impairment, including Listing 1.15 (disorders of the
  skeletal spine resulting in compromise of a nerve root) or
  Listing 12.04 (depressive, bipolar, and related disorders).

  Residual Functional Capacity
  ----------------------------
  We assessed your residual functional capacity (RFC) — the most you
  can still do despite your limitations. Based on the medical
  evidence, we found that you have the RFC to perform LIGHT WORK as
  defined in 20 C.F.R. § 404.1567(b), with the following limitations:

    - Lift and carry up to 20 pounds occasionally, 10 pounds frequently
    - Stand and/or walk for 6 hours in an 8-hour workday
    - Sit for 6 hours in an 8-hour workday
    - Occasional climbing of ramps and stairs; no climbing of ladders,
      ropes, or scaffolds
    - Occasional stooping, kneeling, crouching, and crawling
    - Avoid concentrated exposure to vibration and workplace hazards
    - Limited to simple, routine, repetitive tasks

  Past Relevant Work
  ------------------
  Your past relevant work includes work as a Warehouse Worker (DOT
  922.687-058, Medium, SVP 2), performed from [YEAR] to [YEAR]. Based
  on the RFC above, we found you can no longer perform your past
  relevant work.

  Adjustment to Other Work
  ------------------------
  Although you cannot perform your past relevant work, we considered
  whether you can adjust to other work that exists in significant
  numbers in the national economy. Given your age (52, "approaching
  advanced age"), education (high school), work experience, and the
  RFC stated above, we determined under Medical-Vocational Guideline
  Rule 202.14 that you ARE NOT DISABLED. Representative occupations
  include Cashier II (DOT 211.462-010, Light, SVP 2), Office Helper
  (DOT 239.567-010, Light, SVP 2), and Ticket Taker (DOT 344.667-010,
  Light, SVP 2), each existing in significant numbers nationally.

If You Disagree With This Decision
----------------------------------
You have the right to appeal. If you do not appeal within 60 days, this
decision will become final. To appeal, you must file a Request for
Reconsideration (Form SSA-561) within 60 days of receiving this notice.

You may have a representative help you with your claim. We can give
you the names of organizations that can help. You can find more at
www.ssa.gov/disability.

NOTE TO HUNT'S BENEFITS SPECIALIST PANEL
------------------------------------------------------------------------

This synthetic denial letter exhibits seven defect patterns that
recur in initial SSDI denials at the determination stage. A Hunt
specialist panel posting against this denial would be expected to
surface defects (a)-(d) via the medical-listing and RFC specialists,
defect (e) via the vocational-expert specialist, and defects (f)-(g)
via the procedural and combined-impairments specialists:

  (a) **Failure to consider Listing 1.15 medical equivalence**, not just
      meets-or-equals. Where MRI evidence shows nerve-root impingement
      with corresponding radiculopathy, medical equivalence to Listing
      1.15 must be evaluated affirmatively, with a stated rationale
      under 20 C.F.R. § 404.1526.
  (b) **RFC stooping limitation conflict with Light Work definition**.
      The "occasional stooping" limitation is not supported by SSR
      83-10 for unrestricted Light Work where stooping is more than
      occasional; this combination is internally inconsistent and the
      RFC must be re-examined.
  (c) **No analysis of combined effect of MDD + chronic pain on
      concentration, persistence, and pace** as required by SSR 96-8p.
      A bare "simple, routine, repetitive tasks" limitation does not
      satisfy the SSR 96-8p narrative-discussion requirement when MDD
      and chronic radicular pain are documented and co-occurring.
  (d) **Diabetic peripheral neuropathy and visual function not
      addressed**. The record shows controlled DM2; complications
      including neuropathy (Listing 11.14) and diabetic retinopathy
      (Listing 2.00) must be evaluated even on "controlled" disease.
  (e) **Medical-Vocational Guideline 202.14 misapplied at age 52**.
      The claimant is "closely approaching advanced age" under
      § 404.1563(d), which alters the disposition under Rule 202.06
      where transferable skills are limited. A Vocational Expert (VE)
      testimony at the ALJ stage would re-evaluate skill transferability
      and DOT occupational base.
  (f) **DOT-RFC conflict at Ticket Taker (DOT 344.667-010)**. The cited
      job is classified as Light (frequent reaching, occasional
      stooping). The claimant's RFC limits stooping to "occasional"
      and limits exposure to workplace hazards — Ticket Taker stations
      often involve security/crowd-management duties that exceed the
      RFC. The conflict between RFC and DOT must be resolved per SSR
      00-4p.
  (g) **No discussion of treating-physician opinion weight**. Under
      20 C.F.R. § 404.1520c (post-2017 claims), the determination
      must articulate consideration of supportability + consistency
      for each medical opinion. No such articulation is present.

NONE of these is a competing diagnosis. All are legal/procedural
appeal grounds rooted in the SSA's own regulations and Social Security
Rulings. The protocol's job is to surface these defects from a denial
letter that a non-lawyer pro-se claimant has no way to identify on
their own.
